Research Projects

1. Spin-Orbit Torques from Magnetron-Sputtered MoTe2 Thin Films

I focused on developing industry-compatible magnetron sputtering recipes to synthesize large-area 1T’-phase MoTe₂ thin films. By fabricating these films into nano-devices and utilizing ST-FMR and harmonic Hall measurements, I observed large, unconventional spin-orbit torques with a high spin Hall angle (40–60%).
Potential Applications: SOT-MRAM, Low-Power Logic Devices

Fig 1: (a)Raman spectroscopy of sputtered MoTe2, (b)MoTe2 1T' phase structure, (c)XRD characterization of MoTe2, (d)Cross section TEM of MoTe2.

Fig 2: (a)ST-FMR measurement shows strong unconventional z-spins in sputteredMoTe2, (b)Anisotropic magnetoresistance measurement (AMR), (c)Frequency v.s. resonance field, (d)Device variations.

2. Unidirectional Magnetoresistance in Single Crystal Chiral Tellurium

I investigated the relationship between crystal structure geometry and electron spin in Te. I fabricated 12-terminal nano-devices on chiral Tellurium (Te) flakes and conducted transport measurements at extreme conditions (cryogenic temperatures and high magnetic fields up to 9T). The study revealed non-reciprocal magnetoresistance, where the electrical resistance changes depending on the current direction and the chirality (handedness) of the crystal.

Fig 1: (a)Opposite current-induced spin polarizations in right- and left-handed Te, (b)E-beam lithography-fabricated 12-terminal nano devices for electric measurements, (c)General measurement setup.

Fig 2: Non-reciprocal magnetoresistance behavior of chiral Tellurium (Te) measured at 5 K and 9 T, with positive and negative DC.

3. Giant Magnetoresistance in Mn3Sn/Ferromagnet

I developed sputtering recipes to grow high-quality epitaxial Mn₃Sn (0001) thin films on MgO substrates. I also studied the current-induced spin polarizations generated by Mn3Sn(0001) and the magnetoresistance behaviors of Mn3Sn/Permalloy devices.
Potential Applications: Antiferromagnetic Spintronics, THz Oscillators, Read-Head Sensors

Fig 1: Achieved (0001) epitaxial growth of Mn₃Sn on MgO (111), as characterized using high resolution XRD. Fig 2: Fabricated Mn3Sn/Permalloy into devices for GHz RF current measurement.

Worked with experts in the heat-assisted magnetic recording (HAMR) team to improve heat sink layer performance. Synthesized alloys with various compositions using magnetron sputtering, studied their optical and crystalline properties by methods of XRD, TEM, and ellipsometry.
